## Summary
Wire architect-emitted `research_needs` into the planning workflows so any architect at any depth can declare information gaps, have the research sub-workflow (#3109) run, and resume on the same architect node with findings available — without traversing the plan-review gate. Loop is bounded by a counter modeled on the existing `open_questions_counter` pattern in [.conductor/registry/workflows/plan-level.yaml](.conductor/registry/workflows/plan-level.yaml).

## Problem / Motivation
Architects today have no first-class mechanism to say "I cannot plan this responsibly without investigating X". Sibling Issues add a `researcher` agent (#3109) and storage abstraction (#3108), but without an architect-side emission channel and workflow routing those facilities are unreachable from the planning loop. Re-using the plan-review gate for this would force a human-in-the-loop on every research cycle, defeating the point of an autonomous research escape hatch.

## Proposed Approach
Three changes, layered:

1. **Architect contract** — extend the architect output schema in [.conductor/registry/workflows/plan-level.yaml](.conductor/registry/workflows/plan-level.yaml) (`architect` step, alongside `plan`, `children`, `open_questions`, `summary`) with a new `research_needs[]` array. Each entry: `topic`, `question`, `why_it_matters`, optional `suggested_sources`. Update [.conductor/registry/prompts/architect-plan-level.md](.conductor/registry/prompts/architect-plan-level.md) and [.polyphony-config/agent-guidance/architect.md](.polyphony-config/agent-guidance/architect.md) to document when to emit and how the field interacts with `open_questions` (research_needs = "I will investigate then re-plan"; open_questions = "I need a human to decide").

2. **Workflow routing & bounded loop** — in `plan-level.yaml`, after the `architect` step (and before `open_questions_policy`), add:
   - `research_needs_counter` — modeled on `open_questions_counter`, computes iteration & cap_reached against a `max_research_loops` policy value (default cap, e.g. 3).
   - Conditional route: when `architect.output.research_needs | length > 0` and not cap_reached, invoke the research sub-workflow (#3109) as a `subworkflow` step, then route back to `architect` (re-entry) — bypassing `open_questions_policy`, plan-PR, and `plan_reviewer` entirely.
   - Findings are delivered to the next architect iteration via the same channel #3109 publishes (likely a `research_findings` document path threaded through context, mirroring how `ancestor_chain` is exposed).
   - cap_reached routes to a `research_cap_gate` (human_gate, mirrors `open_questions_gate` cap behavior) so a misbehaving architect cannot loop forever.

3. **Verification** — harness scenario in [tests/harness/scenarios/](tests/harness/scenarios/) that scripts an architect emitting `research_needs` once, asserts the research sub-workflow ran, asserts the plan-review gate did NOT run, and asserts re-entry into `architect` with findings available. A second scenario asserts the bound: 4 consecutive emissions trip `research_cap_gate`. End-to-end dogfood demo recorded against this Epic's own remaining children once #3108/#3109 are merged.

**Out of scope (per parent #3107):** wiki polish (#3113), apex close-out scratch pruning under `research/scratch/` (stretch — flagged as open question below).

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Architect output schema declares `research_needs[]` and is documented in the architect prompt.
- [ ] `plan-level.yaml` routes `research_needs` to the research sub-workflow and re-enters `architect` without traversing `open_questions_policy`, plan PR, or `plan_reviewer` (verified by harness scenario).
- [ ] Bounded loop: a configurable `max_research_loops` (default 3) caps consecutive research cycles per planning level; exceeding it surfaces a human gate.
- [ ] End-to-end demo: an architect declares `research_needs`, receives synthesized findings from #3109, and produces a refined plan in its next iteration.
- [ ] Build passes with zero errors and warnings.
- [ ] All existing tests pass; new harness scenarios cover bypass and bound behaviors.
