# Plan — `archivist` curation pass + sibling-repo write (AB#3135)

## Summary
Append an `archivist` curation step (keep / discard / expand) as the terminal stage of every research call, and wire the sibling-repo writer on top of the storage abstraction delivered by AB#3132. Kept articles land in the sibling research wiki with Johnny-Decimal paths and minimal frontmatter, and `INDEX.md` is updated as part of the same write. Deep wiki polish (frontmatter schema, wiki-link resolver, deterministic INDEX regen, citation validator) is owned by sibling AB#3137 — this issue delivers the minimum write surface those validators will then enforce.

## Problem / Motivation
The research sub-workflow shipped in AB#3133 returns synthesized findings but has no terminal disposition: nothing decides whether the result is worth keeping, and nothing graduates kept articles from scratch (`research/scratch/<run>/` on the feature branch) to the sibling research repo. Without curation, every run accumulates dead scratch and zero durable knowledge; the wiki-polish work in AB#3137 has nothing to polish until kept articles actually arrive.

## Proposed Approach
Append two structurally separate stages to the research sub-workflow built in AB#3133, both at the terminal end (disjoint from the mid-loop escalation step that AB#3134 inserts):

1. **`archivist` agent** — runs once per research call after the researcher/deep-researcher finishes. Reads the synthesized findings + the scratch artefact and emits a structured `{disposition: keep|discard|expand, rationale, title?, johnny_decimal_hint?}` decision. `expand` short-circuits to a follow-up research call (re-enters the sub-workflow with a refined `research_needs`); `discard` is a no-op terminal state (scratch remains, pruned at apex close); `keep` triggers the writer.
2. **Sibling-repo writer** — a deterministic script step that consumes the `keep` decision plus the findings document and writes a single article to the sibling research repo via the storage abstraction from AB#3132. Path is Johnny-Decimal (`<area>/<category>/<id>-<slug>.md`), with **minimal** frontmatter (`title`, `captured_at`, `source_run`, `sources[]`); the full frontmatter schema is declared and enforced by AB#3144. `INDEX.md` is updated by appending the new article entry in a stable section — the deterministic full-regen is AB#3147's job.

Cross-cutting:
- Reuse the storage abstraction surface from AB#3132 verbatim — no new credential plumbing.
- The archivist's output schema is a stable contract consumed by both the writer and AB#3137's downstream validators; freeze it in this issue.
- All three insertion points (archivist step, writer step, INDEX append) sit at the terminal end of the sub-workflow YAML, structurally disjoint from AB#3134's mid-loop escalation insertion.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Every research call ends with an `archivist` agent invocation that emits a structured keep/discard/expand decision.
- [ ] `keep` decisions write exactly one article to the sibling research repo via the AB#3132 storage abstraction.
- [ ] Kept articles use a Johnny-Decimal path and carry minimal frontmatter (`title`, `captured_at`, `source_run`, `sources[]`) such that AB#3144's schema validator will pass on them.
- [ ] `INDEX.md` is updated (appended) in the same write as the article — no separate run required for first appearance.
- [ ] `discard` writes nothing to the sibling repo; `expand` re-enters the sub-workflow with a refined `research_needs` and does not write.
- [ ] A harness scenario exercises all three dispositions end-to-end against an in-memory storage backend.
- [ ] Build passes with zero errors and warnings.
- [ ] All existing tests pass; new tests cover archivist decisioning, the writer's path/frontmatter shape, and the INDEX append.
